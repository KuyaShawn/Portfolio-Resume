Personal Statement

Essay Prompt:

             Being born as a foreigner and being from a low-income family household. I wouldn’t have dreamt of being in college and having an “American Dream”, not until I’ve realized that I can change that dream and turn it into reality. When I was the age of three, I moved from the Philippines to the US with my mother, my father had the money to bring us here to the US as his side of the family was well off. But because my father was the only one who was generating the income, it was hard for us to only depend on him, so my mother took her time to find a job while also trying her best to teach me some English words that she remembered learned from school so that when I start school I would be at least ready to communicate with other students and the teacher. As I started elementary school, I had a hard time making friends and communicating with them due to a language barrier and as kids, some would make fun of my accent and my inability of speaking English, so they placed me into an ESL class and within time and practice I’ve gotten better with my English and understanding the things around me. One day my parent gifted me my first device with a few games, the console was called a Nintendo DS. As this was my first console, the first thing I did to it was to fidget around with it and learn how to make it work since I had no clue on how to use it, when I figured out how to use it I was very excited. One day I was curious about how this device worked so I played around with it until it broke, I was a little saddened that my DS broke, but my father showed me that things could always be fixed, so he demonstrated to me how to replace a battery on the DS. At that moment that’s when I caught on to learning how to fix things by replacing the batteries off non-working devices like a remote.

            During my time in middle school, I took a computer class that guided us on how to use the Windows operating system, the internet, and how to secure yourself from malicious programs and scammers. This class is what sparked my interest in how computers worked, and because I liked fixing things, I’ve always wondered how people fixed things within the internet. So then, I came looking for answers, and as you know the almighty google has shown me careers that’s associated with computers and the internet, as a kid I was fascinated with the word “hacker” and I learned that cybersecurity was that hacker job that fits my desired career. But as time goes on, I’ve become more familiar with how the Operating systems and internet works, and during my spare time after school, I would try to learn how to code through google chrome’s developer tool. As I continue to practice and use this tool, I become more interested in wanting to develop my own apps and software. And so, I’ve made it a goal after high school that I would become a software developer. Not only that I’ve always had it as a goal to make my parents proud for their hard work, and I’ve always wanted to repay that hardship by showing them I will succeed and help make their lives easier with my future career, as I plan on developing an app that would help benefit them. This Is when I realized that my dream is becoming more of a reality as I slowly step forward closer to it. There will be roadblocks achieving this dream, but I know for a fact that It will be worth it at the end.
